WHO WE ARE?

Chemineers Society is a student body aiming to promote intellectual and cultural activities for the students of the Department of Chemical Engineering

What we do?

Our society continuously organizes several activities to promote cultural interactions and overall development of our department's students.

What we achieve?

We help students identify campus resources and foster harmonious relationships among the students, faculty, and alumni of the CHE department and ensure that there is no communication gap between them.

How do we do it?

The society conducts various departmental events. These events may include the Happy Hours, Technical workshops, Freshers Party, Alumni Talks, Farewell Party, Sports tournaments, etc

At the end ?

We try our best to achieve our aim to promote intellectual and cultural activities for the students of the Department of Chemical Engineering

SEVESVO DISASTER

Seveso became one of the world’s deadliest industrial tragedies, igniting an urgent outcry against the chemical sector for tighter safety laws. Seveso triggered the implementation of so-called “EU-Seveso” guidelines in Europe, first in 1982 and then again in 1996, aiming at the prevention and management of such incidents. A chemical factory explosion near Seveso, Italy, in July 1976, exposed residents to the highest known amounts of 2,3,7,8-tetrachlorodibenzo-p-dioxin (TCDD or dioxin) exposure in a residential population.

GREEN HYDROGEN

The first green hydrogen fueling station in India is being installed in Leh, Union Territory of Ladakh. The plant is expected to be operational by May of next year[1]. It is a one-of-a-kind project being built by the state-owned NTPC. Green Hydrogen, obtained by electrolysis from renewable electricity (which separates hydrogen from the oxygen present in water)[2], is positioned as one of the most important alternatives to traditional energy resources and helps us drive towards decarbonization of transport and sustainable mobility.

2-D GRAPHITE

Graphene is 2D fabric material composed of a thick hexagonal layer. This fabric has attracted tremendous interest amongst clinical networks due to its excessive surface area, fantastic mechanical properties, and conductivity because of unfastened electrons in its lattice. There are various approaches to prepare its nanosheets, such as the top-down approach. The bottom-up technique involves hydrocarbon deposition via CVD(Chemical Vapour Deposition),organo-synthesis, etc. In the present scene, the top-down technique is used to put together graphene.
